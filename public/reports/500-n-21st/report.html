7. The deciding risk — by-room legality & the casita

Do not treat the ~$4,500-5,600/mo room income as a given. This is a long-term (30+ day) rental, so it is not the banned short-term-rental (STR) use, and Nevada sets no cap on unrelated occupants. BUT the City of Las Vegas (Title 19) has a narrow "Boarding/Rooming House" use (≤4 non-family for compensation) that is restricted/deleted in several single-family zones — renting 6 rooms to 6 unrelated strangers can be construed as a rooming house / commercial use that may not be permitted at this parcel's zoning without a special-use permit, and could draw code enforcement. Your living on-site in the casita helps the "owner renting spare rooms" framing but does not settle it.

Gate the purchase on written answers from: City of LV Planning/Zoning (is long-term by-room rental an allowed use at this parcel?), Business Licensing (495 S. Main St — what license, if any), and confirmation the casita is a permitted, finished second dwelling. If by-room is disallowed, the fallback is whole-house rent of only ~$2,600-3,300/mo, which does not cover a $505K purchase — so this answer decides the deal.
